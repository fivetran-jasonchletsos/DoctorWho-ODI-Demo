Rogues' Gallery

Monsters & Recurring Foes

Pepperpots from Skaro. Cyber-converted Mondasians. Stone angels you mustn't blink at. The chained jackal-god under the Pyramid of Mars. The Master, in whatever face this week.

Dalek-adjacent

110+ stories

Daleks

Debut: The Daleks (1963) · Skaro

Threat

Signature.Plunger. Whisk. "EX-TER-MIN-ATE." Pepperpot.

Davros's hate-engines. Show's longest-running enemy. Time War kicked it up; Genesis of the Daleks remains the franchise's moral centre. "Have I that right?"

Weakness.Their own purity protocols, every time. Bracewell. The Hand of Omega. The Moment.

Cyber

60+ stories

Cybermen

Debut: The Tenth Planet (1966) · Mondas (and Cybus parallel)

Threat

Signature.Stomp. Tear tracks etched into the helmet. "You will be upgraded."

Earth's lost twin. Mondas, Telos, Cybus Industries, the Cyberiad. Killed Adric, killed Bill, killed the Brigadier and made him salute the Doctor anyway.

Weakness.Gold (classic), emotional override (modern), the Master (Doctor Falls).

Time Lord

50+ stories

The Master / Missy

Debut: Terror of the Autons (1971) · Gallifrey

Threat

Signature.Pinstripe, goatee (classic) / parasol, Mary-Poppins-with-a-Glock (Missy) / The drumbeat (Simm) / Big Finish all of the above.

The Doctor's oldest friend, oldest enemy. Roger Delgado, Anthony Ainley, Eric Roberts, John Simm, Michelle Gomez, Sacha Dhawan. Drumbeats in the head from a Time Lord wormhole.

Other

15+ stories

Sontarans

Debut: The Time Warrior (1973) · Sontar

Threat

Signature.Potato-shaped clone warriors. Probic vent at the back of the neck.

Eleven thousand year war with the Rutans. Honour above sense. "Sontar-ha!" Strax is one of theirs — domesticated.

Weakness.A well-aimed shot to the probic vent.

Dalek-adjacent

11+ stories

Davros

Debut: Genesis of the Daleks (1975) · Skaro

Threat

Signature.Withered scientist in a Dalek base, single blue eye in the forehead.

The man who made them. Has been retconned from withered Kaled scientist to whole-bodied young man in The Beginning. The franchise's purest villain.

Ancient

8+ stories

Silurians

Debut: Doctor Who and the Silurians (1970) · Earth (pre-human)

Threat

Signature.Reptilian sapients, third eye, predates humanity by a billion years.

The original Earthlings — the Doctor's deepest ethical bind. Madame Vastra is one of theirs, married to Jenny Flint.

Ancient

7+ stories

Weeping Angels

Debut: Blink (2007)

Threat

Signature.Stone statue. Becomes a statue whenever observed. Hands over eyes when desperate. Image of an Angel becomes an Angel.

Send you to the past, feed on the life you would have lived. Don't blink. Don't even blink. Blink and you're dead. Single best monster of the modern era.

Weakness.Quantum lock — keep them under observation by anything sentient, forever.

Other

7+ stories

Ice Warriors

Debut: The Ice Warriors (1967) · Mars

Threat

Signature.Cold-blooded green-armoured Martians; hiss when angry.

Honourable warriors of Mars. Eleven, Twelve and Thirteen all met them; Empress of Mars retconned them as a credible long-term Earth treaty partner.

Other

6+ stories

The Ravagers / Swarm and Azure

Debut: The Halloween Apocalypse (2021)

Threat

Signature.White-faced cosmic siblings who hate Time and want to end it.

Flux antagonists. Personifications of entropy; the Doctor had locked them up in the Division's past.

Other

5+ stories

The Silence

Debut: The Impossible Astronaut (2011)

Threat

Signature.Edvard Munch's Scream in a suit. You forget them the moment you look away.

A religious order, not a species (Day of the Moon). Engineered Melody Pond to kill the Doctor. "Silence will fall when the question is asked."

Weakness.Hypnotic suggestion: "You should kill us all on sight."

Great Intelligence

5+ stories

The Great Intelligence

Debut: The Abominable Snowmen (1967)

Threat

Signature.Disembodied mind; takes hosts (snow, snowmen, Walter Simeon, Yetis).

Two and Eleven and Twelve fought it across decades. Walked into the Doctor's timestream in Name of the Doctor and broke him into pieces only Clara could put back together.

Domestic

5+ stories

The Ood

Debut: The Impossible Planet (2006) · Ood-Sphere

Threat

Signature.Coral-pink head, translation orb on a tether, hindbrain in the palm.

Sold as servants because humans cut out their hindbrains. Free Ood are gentle. Ood Sigma — "Doctor-Donna, your song must end soon." Their songs killed Ten by knocking four times.

Other

4+ stories

Zygons

Debut: Terror of the Zygons (1975) · Zygor

Threat

Signature.Orange suction-cup amphibians; shapeshift via a body-print.

Brilliant single-serial monster in 1975, returned in 50th anniversary, got the Day of the Doctor / Zygon Inversion two-parter that gave Twelve his "war speech."

4+ stories

The Trickster

Debut: Whatever Happened to Sarah Jane? (2007)

Threat

Signature.Robed trickster; offers people second chances and weaponises the changes.

Sarah Jane Adventures villain. Brought into the main show by Turn Left — every wrong choice in Donna's life can be traced to one of his bugs on her back.

Other

4+ stories

Slitheen / Raxacoricofallapatorians

Debut: Aliens of London (2005) · Raxacoricofallapatorius

Threat

Signature.Big green babies in human suits, joints unzipped at the forehead. Excessive farting.

Family of criminal Raxacoricofallapatorians. The Slitheen are one family; Blathereen are another. Sarah Jane Adventures villains, mostly.

Ancient

4+ stories

Sea Devils

Debut: The Sea Devils (1972) · Earth

Threat

Signature.Underwater Silurian cousins; net-helmets and bone-coloured armour.

The aquatic branch. Returned for Legend of the Sea Devils with Thirteen — gave us TARDIS-as-pirate-ship.

The Rani

Debut: The Mark of the Rani (1985) · Gallifrey

Threat

Signature.Ruthless scientist Time Lady; uses people as test subjects.

Amoral lab-coat counterpart to the Master. Mark Strickson said her TV outings under-served the character; the audios reclaim her. Long-rumoured returnee.

2+ stories

The Mara

Debut: Kinda (1982)

Threat

Signature.Snake. Lives in your mind. Tegan's recurring nightmare.

Buddhist-influenced two-parter monster; one of classic Who's weirdest. Possessed Tegan twice across two seasons.

2+ stories

Sutekh

Debut: Pyramids of Mars (1975) · Phaester Osiris

Threat

Signature.Jackal-headed Osirian god of death in eternal chains.

Tom Baker's scariest villain. Returned in Empire of Death — riding on the outside of the TARDIS, unnoticed, since 1975. Killed everything.

The Toymaker

Debut: The Celestial Toymaker (1966)

Threat

Signature.Immortal trickster god in a hall of games; loser becomes a doll.

Returned in The Giggle (2023) to laugh the world into chaos. Tossed away "with a screwdriver and ladybirds." The Pantheon of Discord begins with him.

Threat

Signature.Horned devil chained inside a planet orbiting a black hole.

Older than the universe. Claimed to be the Devil that birthed every Devil. Ten met him at the bottom of a pit and refused to give him a name.

Threat

Signature.Piranhas of the air. Live in shadows; you get two shadows just before they bite.

"Hey. Who turned out the lights?" The Library massacred them; River Song saved 4,022 people into the hard drive.

The Family of Blood

Debut: Human Nature (2007)

Threat

Signature.Possessed bodies of an Edwardian schoolboy, schoolmistress, scarecrow soldiers.

Three-month-living gas family hunted the Doctor; he hid as John Smith and forgot himself for love. Punishments were biblical: scarecrow, mirror, frozen in time.

The Toclafane

Debut: Utopia (2007)

Threat

Signature.Floating chrome spheres; childlike voices; "so old".

The last of humanity from the end of the universe, cannibalised back. The Master used them to invade their own past on a paradox engine in the TARDIS.

Threat

Signature.Voice that copies you, then pre-empts you, then takes you.

Never seen. Never named. Beat the Doctor by stealing his words. Single best 45 minutes of horror the show has ever made.
