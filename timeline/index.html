Timeline

1963–present

63 years of one show. The wilderness years (1990–2004) are the gap nobody talks about — but the books, audios, comics, and the McGann TV movie fill it. We mark them here.

1963

Active:1 (William Hartnell)

  • An Unearthly Child— The first episode. The TARDIS leaves the junkyard at 76 Totter's Lane.
  • The Daleks— Daleks debut, in episode two. The show was made.
1964

Active:1 (William Hartnell)

  • The Aztecs— Establishes "You can't change history. Not one line."
1965

Active:1 (William Hartnell)

1966

Active:1 (William Hartnell) · 2 (Patrick Troughton)

  • The Tenth Planet— First regeneration. First Cybermen.
1967

Active:2 (Patrick Troughton)

  • The Tomb of the Cybermen— Patrick Troughton at his best; sole surviving Troughton Cyber-serial.
1968

Active:2 (Patrick Troughton)

1969

Active:2 (Patrick Troughton)

  • The War Games— Time Lords named for the first time. The Doctor is tried by his own people.
1970

Active:3 (Jon Pertwee)

  • Spearhead from Space— First colour serial. UNIT era proper.
  • Inferno— Parallel-Earth fascism episode.
1971

Active:3 (Jon Pertwee)

  • Terror of the Autons— The Master's first appearance.
1972

Active:3 (Jon Pertwee)

1973

Active:3 (Jon Pertwee)

1974

Active:3 (Jon Pertwee) · 4 (Tom Baker)

1975

Active:4 (Tom Baker)

  • Genesis of the Daleks— Origin of the Daleks. Davros debut. "Have I that right?"
  • Pyramids of Mars— Best classic-era horror tone. Setup paid off 49 years later in Empire of Death.
1976

Active:4 (Tom Baker)

  • The Deadly Assassin— First Gallifrey serial without companions. Cemented Time Lord lore.
1977

Active:4 (Tom Baker)

1978

Active:4 (Tom Baker)

1979

Active:4 (Tom Baker)

  • City of Death— Co-written by Douglas Adams. Highest-rated classic Who episode.
1980

Active:4 (Tom Baker)

1981

Active:4 (Tom Baker) · 5 (Peter Davison)

  • Logopolis— Tom Baker's regeneration.
1982

Active:5 (Peter Davison)

  • Earthshock— Cybermen return after seven years. Adric dies. Credits run in silence.
1983

Active:5 (Peter Davison)

1984

Active:5 (Peter Davison) · 6 (Colin Baker)

  • The Caves of Androzani— Davison's regeneration. Often voted #1 classic Who episode of all time.
1985

Active:6 (Colin Baker)

  • Vengeance on Varos— Reality-TV satire 15 years before reality TV.
1986

Active:6 (Colin Baker)

1987

Active:7 (Sylvester McCoy)

1988

Active:7 (Sylvester McCoy)

  • Remembrance of the Daleks— Coal Hill 1963. Ace destroys a Dalek with a baseball bat.
1989

Active:7 (Sylvester McCoy)

  • The Curse of Fenric— Peak Cartmel Masterplan. Ace gets a proper backstory.
  • Survival— Last classic-era serial. Ends with the Doctor's voice over a sunset.
1990

Active:7 (Sylvester McCoy)

1991

Active:7 (Sylvester McCoy)

1992

Active:7 (Sylvester McCoy)

Active:7 (Sylvester McCoy)

Active:7 (Sylvester McCoy) · 8 (Paul McGann)

Active:8 (Paul McGann)

Active:8 (Paul McGann)

Active:8 (Paul McGann)

2001

Active:8 (Paul McGann)

Active:8 (Paul McGann)

2003

Active:8 (Paul McGann)

2004

Active:8 (Paul McGann)

2005

Active:8 (Paul McGann) · 9 (Christopher Eccleston) · 10 (David Tennant)

  • Rose— The show comes back. RTD's reboot. 10 million viewers.
  • Dalek— Single Dalek in a basement. Nine breaks.
  • The Empty Child / The Doctor Dances— "Are you my mummy?" Steven Moffat's first script.
  • The Parting of the Ways— Eccleston's regeneration. Bad Wolf reveal.
  • The Christmas Invasion— Tennant's first proper episode.
2006

Active:8 (Paul McGann) · 10 (David Tennant)

  • School Reunion— Sarah Jane returns. Sets up her spin-off.
  • The Girl in the Fireplace— Steven Moffat one-shot. Madame de Pompadour and the clockwork droids.
  • Doomsday— Daleks vs Cybermen. Rose says goodbye on Bad Wolf Bay.
2007

Active:8 (Paul McGann) · 10 (David Tennant)

  • Blink— Weeping Angels debut. The Doctor barely in his own episode.
  • Human Nature / The Family of Blood— The Doctor hides as a human and forgets.
  • Utopia / The Sound of Drums / Last of the Time Lords— Master returns. Year That Never Was.
2008

Active:8 (Paul McGann) · 10 (David Tennant)

  • Silence in the Library / Forest of the Dead— First appearance of River Song. Her ending, his beginning.
  • Midnight— Cheap episode forced by budget cuts; one of the best.
  • Turn Left— What if Donna had turned right? Rose returns.
  • The Stolen Earth / Journey's End— Every modern companion in one room. DoctorDonna.
2009

Active:8 (Paul McGann) · 10 (David Tennant)

  • The End of Time— Tennant's regeneration. Four knocks.
2010

Active:8 (Paul McGann) · 10 (David Tennant) · 11 (Matt Smith)

  • The Eleventh Hour— Matt Smith's debut.
  • Vincent and the Doctor— Tony Curran as Vincent van Gogh.
  • The Pandorica Opens / The Big Bang— Series 5 finale. The Pandorica is built for the Doctor.
2011

Active:8 (Paul McGann) · 10 (David Tennant) · 11 (Matt Smith)

  • The Doctor's Wife— Neil Gaiman script. The TARDIS speaks.
  • A Good Man Goes to War— Melody Pond reveal.
2012

Active:8 (Paul McGann) · 10 (David Tennant) · 11 (Matt Smith)

  • The Angels Take Manhattan— Goodbye Ponds.
2013

Active:8 (Paul McGann) · War (John Hurt) · 10 (David Tennant) · 11 (Matt Smith)

  • The Night of the Doctor— McGann returns for seven minutes; turns the War Doctor canon.
  • An Adventure in Space and Time— BBC docudrama about the making of Doctor Who, 1963. David Bradley as William Hartnell.
  • The Day of the Doctor— 50th anniversary. Three Doctors. War Doctor canonised. Gallifrey saved.
  • The Time of the Doctor— Eleven's regeneration. Trenzalore. New regeneration cycle.
2014

Active:10 (David Tennant) · 12 (Peter Capaldi)

  • Listen— Moffat's best psychological one. Or scariest. Same thing.
2015

Active:10 (David Tennant) · 12 (Peter Capaldi)

  • Heaven Sent— Capaldi solo. 55 minutes inside the confession dial. 4.5 billion years.
  • Hell Bent— Gallifrey returns. Clara escapes.
  • Heaven Sent / Hell Bent / Face the Raven— Series 9 finale trilogy. Clara dies, then escapes.
2016

Active:10 (David Tennant) · 12 (Peter Capaldi)

2017

Active:10 (David Tennant) · 12 (Peter Capaldi)

  • World Enough and Time / The Doctor Falls— Mondasian Cybermen origin. Missy and Master duel.
  • Twice Upon a Time— Capaldi's regeneration. First Doctor cameo (David Bradley).
2018

Active:10 (David Tennant) · 13 (Jodie Whittaker)

  • The Woman Who Fell to Earth— Jodie Whittaker's debut.
  • Demons of the Punjab— Partition of India, 1947. Yaz's grandmother.
2019

Active:10 (David Tennant) · 13 (Jodie Whittaker)

2020

Active:10 (David Tennant) · 13 (Jodie Whittaker)

  • Spyfall— Sacha Dhawan's Master debut. Gallifrey destroyed (again).
  • Fugitive of the Judoon— Jo Martin's Fugitive Doctor. Canon explodes.
  • The Timeless Children— Timeless Child reveal. Doctor predates Gallifrey.
2021

Active:10 (David Tennant) · 13 (Jodie Whittaker)

  • Flux— Six-part serial. The Flux unmakes the universe.
2022

Active:10 (David Tennant) · 13 (Jodie Whittaker)

  • The Power of the Doctor— Whittaker's regeneration. Classic companions return. Bigeneration set up.
2023

Active:10 (David Tennant) · 14 (David Tennant) · 15 (Ncuti Gatwa)

  • The Star Beast— Tennant returns. Meep. DoctorDonna problem.
  • Wild Blue Yonder— Two-hander on the edge of the universe.
  • The Giggle— Bigeneration. Two Doctors at once.
  • The Church on Ruby Road— Gatwa's first proper episode.
2024

Active:15 (Ncuti Gatwa)

  • The Devil's Chord— 1963 Abbey Road. Music returns to the world.
  • Boom— Moffat returns. Doctor stands on a landmine.
  • Empire of Death— Sutekh returns after 49 years. Has been on the TARDIS the whole time.
2025

Active:15 (Ncuti Gatwa)

  • The Robot Revolution— Belinda debut.
  • Lux— Mr Ring-a-Ding. Animated villain.
2026

Active:15 (Ncuti Gatwa)
