TARDIS · Tooling

Bigger on the Inside

The Type 40 capsule. The sonic. Psychic paper. K-9. The cloister bell. Every gadget the Doctor reaches for — and the eight console rooms we've seen since 1963.

Gadgets & Tools

Sonic Screwdriver

Canonical

Fury from the Deep (1968) → present

Universal multitool for the Doctor: unlocks, scans, hacks, occasionally heals.

Famously can't open deadlocks, won't work on wood, useless against Daleks. Each Doctor's design is different; Twelve briefly switched to sonic sunglasses. Disliked by writers because it solves too much.

Psychic Paper

The End of the World (2005) → present

Shows the viewer whatever credentials the Doctor wants them to see.

Doesn't work on the genius, the strong-willed or the species-immune. Goes blank in front of Shakespeare. RTD's narrative shortcut around "why are you allowed in here?"

Vortex Manipulator

The Empty Child (2005)

Time travel via wrist-strap. "A cheap and nasty form of time travel."

Captain Jack's. River Song's. Time Agency tech. Hurts to use. The Doctor calls it the cheap version of the TARDIS.

K-9

Canonical

The Invisible Enemy (1977)

Robot dog. Laser nose. Encyclopaedic database. Affirmative.

Four K-9s in canon: K-9 Mark I (left in 5000 AD), Mark II (left in E-Space), Mark III (Sarah Jane's; killed and rebuilt many times), Mark IV (the K-9 series). The original prop is in the National Media Museum.

Chameleon Arch

Human Nature (2007)

Rewrites a Time Lord's biology into a human; stores the original self in a pocket watch.

Used by the Doctor to hide from the Family of Blood. Used by the Master to hide as Professor Yana at the end of the universe. Don't open the watch.

Cloister Bell

Logopolis (1981)

TARDIS warning system. Rings when the universe is ending. Not a metaphor.

If you hear it, run.

Time Vortex Energy

The Parting of the Ways (2005)

Raw fuel of the TARDIS. Will kill anyone who looks into it. Will not kill Rose.

Rose absorbed the whole vortex and brought Jack back to life. Permanently. He'll be the Face of Boe one day.

Stattenheim Remote

The Two Doctors (1985)

Summons the TARDIS to you.

Six had one. The Doctor doesn't really need it because the TARDIS sometimes shows up anyway.

Hadron Energy Manipulator (sonic sunglasses)

The Magician's Apprentice (2015)

Sonic screwdriver, but as sunglasses.

Brief experiment. Mocked. Quietly retired.

Time Ring

Genesis of the Daleks (1975)

Bracelet-form time-and-space recall; emergency yank back to the TARDIS.

Time Lords gave one to Four when they sent him to Skaro to abort the Daleks. It's still nicer than a vortex manipulator.

TARDIS Key

An Unearthly Child (1963)

Opens the police-box door. Sometimes does other things.

Vibrates around the TARDIS. Used as a hypnosis focus in The Daleks' Master Plan. Has been carried as a perception-filtered necklace.

Hand of Omega

Remembrance of the Daleks (1988)

Stellar manipulator. Customises suns.

Hartnell's Doctor took it from Gallifrey. McCoy's Doctor sent it back to lure the Daleks. They activated it. Skaro's sun went nova.

Console Rooms

Doctor 1–6

White roundel walls, hexagonal console, central rotor. The default. Looks like 1963 BBC props because it is.

Doctor 4

Wooden secondary console room — only Tom Baker used it. Gothic. Hangs in the TARDIS somewhere.

Doctor 7–8

Edwardian library / steampunk parlour. Eight's TV-movie console room has a fireplace.

Doctor 9–10

Coral struts, organic feel. The first "modern" console room. "It's bigger on the inside."

Doctor 11

Glass floor, twin rotors, steampunk dials. "Cosy."

Doctor 12

Bookshelves, chalkboards, armchairs. Doctor lives in this one. Sonic sunglasses on the table.

Doctor 13

Crystalline pillars, hexagonal step-down. Custard-cream dispenser.

Doctor 15

Bright yellow trim, large roundels, jukebox, coffee machine. "Coffee shop console room." The TARDIS picked it.
